# Line-of-Code Budget

Status: superseded

Supersedes: `docs/budget-2026-07-05b.md`

Ratified by: `docs/adr/0009-loc-budget-56k.md`; this revision implements the
euler-core growth allocation for the canvas retention ADR
(`docs/adr/canvas-retention-and-auto-compaction-2026-07-06.md`), approved by
Eli 2026-07-06.

Single change from 2026-07-05b: **`euler-core` 11,800 → 12,500 (+700)**.

Rationale: retention slice 1 (token-budget retention, stub demotion, policy
type, off-tier honest failure, telemetry) is mission-core machinery — it
implements the ratified "degrade content, never facts" contract
(`docs/contracts/canvas.md`). The slice deleted the item-count windowing
concept rather than layering on it; euler-core actual moved 11,959 → 12,292.
The +700 covers actuals plus small headroom. Slices 2–3 (structured tier,
policy surfaces) get their own budget revision when they land — this
allocation is NOT pre-approval for their growth. A session.rs consolidation
pass remains queued before v0.2 (session.rs 2,161 lines).

All other rows and rules are unchanged from 2026-07-05b, including:

- `euler-cli` stays at 25,250 and stays red on purpose: standing,
  per-commit-disclosed debt scheduled for post-release TUI consolidation.
  The red euler-cli row is NOT a stop condition for a briefed slice;
  growing euler-cli beyond what the brief requires is. Every commit
  touching euler-cli discloses the number.
- Extension crates are advisory-only (`euler-extension-session-export`,
  `euler-extension-causal-dag`); core boundary rules per the Boundary Test
  still apply.
- Budgets cover non-test Rust source as counted by `scripts/loc_report.sh`
  (excludes `tests/` dirs and `tests.rs`, `*_test.rs`, `*_tests.rs`).

Per-crate allocations are local guardrails, not additive permission to
exceed the binding 56K hard ceiling.
